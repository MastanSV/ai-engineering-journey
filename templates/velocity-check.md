## Velocity check (every Saturday before retro)

Count the week's slips:

- ≥3 days with 🔄, OR
- total carryover > 1 hour

→ **Trigger re-plan.** Choose ONE:

1. **Cut scope** — drop one optional task (e.g., Ridge/Lasso → Week 2)
2. **Use buffer** — Sat/Sun 6hr blocks have slack
3. **Accept smaller week** — ship what you have, log gap honestly in retro

🚫 **Never fake-finish.** A checkmark on incomplete work poisons future planning.

## Sunday retro additions

In addition to standard retro questions, answer:

1. How many days were 🔄 this week?
2. What's the _pattern_ in slip reasons? (always Tue? always after warmup? always when fatigued?)
3. What will I adjust in next week's plan? (lower load? earlier start? cut a task?)
4. Did any 🔄 become "concept debt" (rushed and not fully understood)? Schedule backfill.

## Layered learning method (companion rule)

For every new ML topic, use:

- **L1** Intuition — 5 sentences in your own words
- **L2** Math — formula + symbol cheat sheet + tiny sanity check
- **L3** Code — minimum runnable example, tested on tiny inputs
- **L4** Reflection — what confused me? what's the bridge to next topic?

⚠️ A 🔄 on L3/L4 is acceptable. A 🔄 that **skips L1/L2** is concept debt — schedule backfill.

## Why this system works

- **Calibration:** Honest 🔄 → sharper time estimates by Week 4
- **Pattern detection:** Sunday retro sees trends, not just totals
- **Honesty muscle:** Senior engineers say _"this took 2x longer than I estimated"_ without flinching. Build this now.
- **Quit prevention:** Binary ✅/❌ tracking → one ❌ feels like failure → people quit at Week 4. 3-state tracking → slips are data, not defeat.
